What is HydroLearn?

HydroLearn is a new educational project funded by the National Science Foundation to promote the development and adoption of active-learning resources in the fields of hydrology and water resources engineering. The project builds on previous developments by our team in the HydroViz project.

Using the HydroLearn platform, educators can:

  • Access real world hydrologic data and modeling tools
  • Customize existing learning modules, build new lessons and learning activities, and share with students as course assignments.
  • Access and modify learning outcomes and assessment rubrics that support the new learning resources

HydroLearn will use modern web technologies to provide wide access to learning resources and enable educators to collaboratively develop, customize and share innovative and rich learning material.

Who

​HydroLearn users include:

  • Educators who want to develop, customize and share innovative learning resources in hydrology and water resources
  • Students who want to learn about fundamental and emerging topics in hydrology and water resources engineering using active learning approaches
The project includes an early-adoption fellowship program to engage educators in testing and developing new learning modules using the HydroLearn resources.

Why

HydroLearn addresses the following challenges that face the development and adoption of innovative learning resources for undergraduate education in the fields of Hydrology and Water Resources Engineering:

  • Educators are hindered by the lack of easy-to-use tools to integrate community-based data, models and analysis tools into teaching content and lessons. Enabling seamless accessibility to these resources will (i) enhance educators’ ability to develop student-engaging learning material and reduce the complexity associated with such development, and (ii) provide more authentic contexts for teaching key topics in hydrology and water resources engineering.
  • Despite the increasing availability of case- and problem-based resources that rely on data and modeling-based learning resources, educators are often “turned-off” by the locality (i.e., region- or data-specific) of resources that are developed by others. By providing educators with a mechanism for self-contribution and customization of content, HydroLearn aspires to build a sense of ownership to encourage educators to develop and adopt new teaching innovations.
  • Educators look for resources that meet specific course learning outcomes aligned with their prescribed academic programs. The HydroLearn platform will include guidance for educators to base the development and customization of learning modules on a sound, well-defined set of student learning outcomes. The use of development strategies with a focus on learning outcomes will promote good practices for content design, serve as a motivator for faculty to achieve accreditation criteria whilst also providing student-engaging learning experiences, and thus grow educators’ interest in hydrology engineering educational innovations.

How

  • Building on our team’s collective efforts in HydroViz, HydroShare and Tethys developments, HydroLearn will allow educators to (a) access research advances in hydrology data and modeling resources, (b) tap into web-based technologies and recent developments in hydro-informatics, and (c) collaboratively develop and share innovative active-learning modules.
  • In collaboration with the National Water Center (NWC), the HydroLearn project will provide capacities that enable access to the newly developed National Water Model (NWM), and thus provide educators with opportunities to develop student learning modules that address water problems with immediate community impacts and critical societal impacts such as flood protection and forecasting.
  • With a focus on achieving faculty adoption, HydroLearn follows a “design for sustained adoption” approach with three core elements: interactive development, interactive dissemination, and support of adopters.
  • Following an early-engagement model, HydroLearn provides funded-fellowships to educators, faculty, and early-career scientists from the hydrology and water resources engineering community to participate in the development and testing of the learning resources using the HydroLearn platform.
